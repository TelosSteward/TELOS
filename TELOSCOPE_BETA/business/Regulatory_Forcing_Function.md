## The Regulatory Tsunami

### **California SB 53 (Effective January 1, 2026)**

**What it requires:**
> "Covered entities must demonstrate **active governance mechanisms** for AI systems, including **continuous monitoring** and **documented intervention capabilities**."

**Who it affects:**
- Any company doing business in California (basically everyone)
- Especially: Financial services, healthcare, legal, education
- **All 167 Fortune 500 companies** using LangChain are covered entities

**What "continuous monitoring" means:**
- Not post-hoc log analysis
- Not quarterly audits
- **Real-time alignment tracking** (exactly what TELOS provides)

**Penalty for non-compliance:**
- Fines up to $50K per violation
- Civil liability for AI-caused harm
- Potential injunctions blocking AI deployments

---

### **EU AI Act Article 72 (Template Published February 2026)**

**What it requires:**
> "High-risk AI systems must maintain **systematic oversight**, including **real-time monitoring** of system behavior and **documented risk mitigation measures**."

**Who it affects:**
- Any company with EU operations or EU customers
- LangChain customers: Klarna (Swedish), Vodafone (UK), and many others

**High-risk AI systems include:**
- Customer service agents (decision-making about services)
- Financial analysis agents (credit, investment decisions)
- Healthcare diagnostic agents
- Legal research agents

**Penalty for non-compliance:**
- Up to €35 million or 7% of global annual revenue (whichever is higher)
- That's **$1.12 billion** for a $16B revenue company

---

### **SEC AI Governance Guidance (Expected Q1 2026)**

**Context:**
- SEC has signaled AI governance requirements for public companies
- Focus: "Algorithmic accountability" and "AI risk disclosure"

**What public companies will need:**
- Board-level AI governance oversight
- Documented AI risk management frameworks
- **Proof of continuous monitoring** for AI systems affecting material business processes

**Who it affects:**
- All publicly traded LangChain customers
