## Executive Summary

**Title**: "Telemetric Keys: Quantum-Resistant Cryptographic Audit Trails for AI Governance Systems"

**Intellectual Merit**: Telemetric Keys introduces a novel cryptographic primitive that creates unforgeable audit trails for AI governance decisions using telemetry-only entropy (zero conversation content exposure). Unlike traditional audit logs (tamperable) or blockchain (poor privacy), Telemetric Keys leverages governance telemetry generated by AI oversight systems to create quantum-resistant signatures (SHA3-512 + HMAC-SHA512, 256-bit security) that anyone can independently verify without accessing private data.

**Broader Impact**: Enables safe deployment of AI systems in high-stakes regulated domains (healthcare, defense, financial services) by providing cryptographically verifiable evidence of AI oversight that satisfies HIPAA § 164.312(b), EU AI Act Article 12, and FDA 21 CFR 820.40 compliance requirements.

**Preliminary Results**: 2,000 cryptographic attacks validated (0% success rate): 0/355 signatures forged, 0/400 keys extracted, constant-time operations verified, memory zeroization confirmed.

## Proposed Research (3 Years)

### Year 1: Cryptographic Foundations & Security Hardening

**Objective**: Formalize Telemetric Keys cryptographic primitives and validate security properties

#### Task 1.1: Formal Security Proofs (Months 1-6)
**Goal**: Prove Telemetric Keys achieves standard cryptographic security properties

**Deliverables**:
- Formal definition of telemetry-based signature scheme
- Proof of unforgeability under chosen-message attack (UF-CMA)
- Proof of quantum resistance against Grover's and BHT algorithms
- Proof of forward secrecy (compromise of session K doesn't expose K-1)

**Methods**:
- Game-based cryptographic proofs
- Reduction to SHA3-512 collision resistance
- Quantum adversary modeling

**Personnel**: PI (25% FTE) + Cryptography postdoc (100% FTE)

#### Task 1.2: Professional Security Audit (Months 4-8)
**Goal**: Independent validation by leading cryptographic security firm

**Partner**: Trail of Bits (or equivalent: NCC Group, Kudelski Security)

**Scope**:
- Penetration testing (signature forgery attempts)
- Key extraction attacks (timing analysis, side-channel)
- Implementation review (constant-time operations, memory safety)
- Threat modeling (adversarial capabilities)

**Deliverables**:
- 50-page security audit report
- Remediation of identified vulnerabilities
- Public security disclosure (responsible disclosure timeline)

**Budget**: $50K (Trail of Bits standard engagement)

#### Task 1.3: Extended Adversarial Validation (Months 6-12)
**Goal**: Expand validation from 2,000 to 10,000+ attacks

**Attack Categories**:
- Signature forgery (1,000 additional attempts)
- Key extraction (1,000 timing/side-channel attacks)
- Replay attacks (500 attempts)
- Man-in-the-middle (500 attempts)
- Quantum adversary simulation (1,000 attacks using Grover's oracle)

**Deliverables**:
- 10,000-attack validation dataset (published on Zenodo)
- Statistical analysis update (99.99% CI)
- Comparison with other cryptographic audit systems

**Budget**: $30K (compute, cloud resources, attack generation)

## Research Questions

### RQ1: Cryptographic Security
**Can telemetry-derived entropy provide equivalent security to traditional random number generators?**

**Hypothesis**: Governance telemetry (fidelity scores, drift magnitude, intervention tiers) provides sufficient entropy for cryptographic signatures despite being derived from structured data rather than true randomness.

**Evaluation**:
- Formal entropy analysis (Shannon entropy, min-entropy)
- NIST Statistical Test Suite for randomness
- Attack resistance validation (10,000+ forgery attempts)

### RQ2: Privacy Preservation
**Can unforgeable audit trails be created without exposing conversation content?**

**Hypothesis**: Telemetry-only signatures enable verifiable governance without compromising privacy.

**Evaluation**:
- Zero-knowledge proof construction
- Information-theoretic privacy analysis
- Compliance validation (HIPAA, GDPR, EU AI Act)

### RQ3: Quantum Resistance
**Do SHA3-512-based Telemetric Keys remain secure against quantum adversaries?**

**Hypothesis**: 256-bit SHA3-512 provides 256-bit quantum security (Grover's algorithm reduces to 2^128, still secure).

**Evaluation**:
- Quantum adversary modeling
- Comparison with NIST post-quantum standards
- Hybrid classical-quantum attack simulations

### RQ4: Performance at Scale
**Can Telemetric Keys scale to enterprise deployments (1000+ AI systems, 1M+ daily signatures)?**

**Hypothesis**: Constant-time HMAC-SHA512 enables sub-10ms signature generation and 1000+ signatures/sec verification.

**Evaluation**:
- Latency benchmarks (p50, p95, p99)
- Throughput testing (signatures per second)
- Production pilot measurements

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Quantum Computing Advances Faster Than Expected

**Scenario**: Quantum computers break SHA3-512 before 2035

**Likelihood**: Low (10-15%)

**Mitigation**:
- Hybrid classical-quantum approach (Year 3)
- Monitor NIST post-quantum updates
- Can migrate to Dilithium + Telemetric Keys

### Risk 2: FIPS 140-3 Certification Delayed

**Scenario**: FIPS certification takes >18 months, exceeds grant period

**Likelihood**: Medium (30-40%)

**Mitigation**:
- Only targeting "preparation" in grant period (full cert is Phase 2)
- Engage certification lab early (Year 1)
- Budget contingency for extended testing

### Risk 3: Enterprise Pilots Encounter Deployment Challenges

**Scenario**: Healthcare/defense partners delay deployments

**Likelihood**: Medium (25-35%)

**Mitigation**:
- Secure pilot agreements in Year 1 (not Year 3)
- Over-recruit (5-7 potential partners for 3-5 actual deployments)
- Offer free deployment support
