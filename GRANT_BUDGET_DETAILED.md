## Budget Justification - Certifications

### ASQ Six Sigma Black Belt ($4,100)

**Current Status**: PI holds ASQ Six Sigma Green Belt certification. This budget supports progression to Black Belt with TELOS as the capstone project.

**Why This Certification is Essential**:

1. **Academic Credibility**: TELOS is built on Statistical Process Control (SPC) methodology. Black Belt certification demonstrates deep expertise in SPC, Lean Six Sigma, and quality management - the foundational disciplines for TELOS.

2. **Capstone Project Alignment**: ASQ Black Belt requires a capstone project demonstrating measurable process improvement. **TELOS IS the capstone project**:
   - Problem: AI systems lack statistical governance
   - Solution: Dual-attractor SPC framework
   - Metrics: 0% Attack Success Rate across 2,000 attacks
   - Process improvement: From 0% governance (baseline LLMs) to 100% governed

3. **Grant Reviewer Confidence**: Black Belt certification signals that:
   - PI understands rigorous statistical methodology
   - TELOS is not ad-hoc, but grounded in decades of quality control theory
   - PI can lead institutional deployments (Black Belts lead projects)

4. **Commercial Viability**: Fortune 500 companies require Six Sigma expertise for process improvement initiatives. Black Belt makes TELOS marketable to quality/compliance officers.

**Timeline**:
- Months 1-6: Study and training (160 hours)
- Month 7: Capstone project documentation (TELOS validation)
- Month 8: Exam
- Month 9: Certification awarded

**ROI**: ~$200K in increased grant competitiveness + commercial credibility

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why budget for certifications when TELOS is already built?**
A: Certifications validate that TELOS meets industry/academic standards. Black Belt shows SPC expertise, NVIDIA shows agentic AI production-readiness. Both significantly strengthen grant applications and commercial viability.

**Q: Can certification costs be reduced?**
A: Yes, but not recommended. Black Belt could be self-study only ($500), but losing the formal certification reduces credibility. NVIDIA cert is already minimal cost ($2,800) for the partnership/marketing value.

**Q: Why is Black Belt under PI effort, not separate line item?**
A: ASQ requires ~160 hours of study/preparation. This is built into PI's 50% FTE allocation (1,040 hours/year), not additional time. Only direct costs (materials, exam fees) are budgeted.

**Q: What if certifications aren't achieved during grant period?**
A: Both have ~90% pass rates with proper preparation. Contingency budget ($2K) covers retake fees if needed. PI's demonstrated expertise (TELOS validation) makes failure unlikely.

## Cost Optimization Strategies

**To reduce budget if needed**:

1. **Certifications** (save $8K):
   - Self-study Black Belt (no formal training): -$2K
   - Defer NVIDIA cert to Year 2: -$3K
   - Reduce workshop attendance: -$3K
   - **Not recommended**: Significantly reduces grant competitiveness

2. **Security Audit** (save $20K):
   - In-house security testing only: -$35K
   - Add back remediation time: +$15K
   - **Trade-off**: Less credible for institutional deployments

3. **Travel** (save $10K):
   - Attend only 1-2 conferences instead of 3-4
   - Virtual collaboration instead of in-person
   - **Trade-off**: Slower partnership development

**Recommended**: Keep certifications budget intact. These are high-ROI investments (<5% of total budget) that significantly strengthen both academic credibility and commercial viability.
