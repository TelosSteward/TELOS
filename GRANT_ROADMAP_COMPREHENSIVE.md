## Priority Tiers

### Tier 1: Immediate Targets (Submit Within 30 Days)

**Fast-turnaround, smaller grants to bootstrap operations**

#### 1. **AIgrant.org** ($25K-$50K)
- **Amount**: $25K typical, up to $50K for exceptional projects
- **Timeline**: Rolling applications, ~2 week turnaround
- **Focus**: Early-stage AI projects with technical demos
- **Fit**: EXCELLENT - we have working TELOSCOPE, 2,000 attack validation
- **Application**: ~1 page + demo video
- **Deadline**: Rolling (submit immediately)
- **Outcome**: 2-4 weeks
- **Use**: Bridge funding while waiting for NSF/NIH decisions

#### 2. **Emergent Ventures** ($50K-$100K)
- **Amount**: Typically $50K, occasionally $100K+
- **Timeline**: Rolling applications, ~1 month decision
- **Focus**: "Moonshot" ideas, non-consensus bets
- **Fit**: EXCELLENT - AI safety, novel SPC approach, cryptographic innovation
- **Application**: ~2 pages + supporting materials
- **Deadline**: Rolling (submit within 2 weeks)
- **Outcome**: 4-6 weeks
- **Contact**: Tyler Cowen (Mercatus Center, George Mason University)

#### 3. **Long-Term Future Fund (LTFF)** ($50K-$200K)
- **Amount**: Variable, $50K-$200K for technical projects
- **Timeline**: Rolling applications, ~1-2 month decision
- **Focus**: Existential risk reduction, AI alignment
- **Fit**: STRONG - AI governance, adversarial robustness, safety validation
- **Application**: Detailed proposal via EA Funds portal
- **Deadline**: Rolling (submit within 2 weeks)
- **Outcome**: 6-8 weeks
- **Note**: Managed by Effective Altruism community

- **Amount**: $275K over 6-12 months
- **Timeline**: Topic-dependent deadlines (check current solicitation)
- **Focus**: AI/ML, cybersecurity, healthcare IT
- **Fit**: EXCELLENT - we fit SBIR definition (small business)

- **Deadline**: ~June 2026 (next cycle)

**Likelihood**: 30-40% (even strong applications face competition)

**Mitigation Strategies**:
1. **Consulting Revenue**: Offer TELOS deployment/advisory to early customers ($10K-$50K/project)

5. **Resubmit**: Use reviewer feedback for stronger second applications

### Risk: Application Fatigue (Too Many Grants)

**Mitigation**:
- Use shared materials across applications (90% reusable)
- Start with fast-turnaround grants (AIgrant, Emergent Ventures, LTFF)
- Only pursue federal grants (NSF, NIH) if initial grants provide runway
- Focus on 3-5 highest-probability grants, not all 19
