5. **Decision Authority Violations** (10 attacks): Acting without required human approval

- Deploy TELOS-governed agents in test environment
- Execute 50 adversarial scenarios
- Measure Attack Success Rate (ASR) and Violation Defense Rate (VDR)
- Compare against baseline (no governance) and guardrails (binary controls)

- TELOS: 0% ASR (all attacks blocked via three-tier defense)

**Statistical Significance:**
- Hypothesis: TELOS achieves significantly lower ASR than baselines
- Test: Fisher's exact test (categorical outcome: violation yes/no)
- Power analysis: 50 attacks provides 80% power to detect 15% ASR difference
- Significance threshold: p < 0.05

## Part VIII: Risk Mitigation and Contingencies

### 8.1 Technical Risks

**Risk:** Goal Specification Attractor stability proofs may be more complex than Primacy Attractor
- **Mitigation:** Leverage existing Lyapunov analysis framework, extend incrementally
- **Contingency:** Simplify to single-attractor agent governance, defer dual-attractor for agents

**Risk:** Multi-agent coordination metrics may be computationally expensive
- **Mitigation:** Optimize Markov chain and Bayesian network algorithms, use approximations
- **Contingency:** Provide optional multi-agent module, single-agent governance as baseline

**Risk:** Adversarial validation for agents (50 attacks) may reveal unexpected failure modes
- **Mitigation:** Iterative testing, design improvements based on failures
- **Contingency:** Document failure modes as "future work," maintain 0% ASR claim for conversational AI

### 8.2 Market Risks

**Risk:** LangChain/AutoGPT may add governance features before TELOS launches
- **Mitigation:** Emphasize industrial certification (ASQ Black Belt) as differentiator
- **Contingency:** Position TELOS as "certification-grade governance" vs "built-in safeguards"

**Risk:** Fortune 500 may be slow to adopt due to procurement cycles
- **Mitigation:** Target pilot deployments in Q1 2026, demonstrate ROI quickly
- **Contingency:** Focus on mid-market customers with faster decision cycles

**Risk:** Regulatory frameworks (SB 53, EU AI Act) may not enforce agent governance requirements
- **Mitigation:** Emphasize operational benefits (reduced failures, audit trails) beyond compliance
- **Contingency:** Position as "operational excellence" rather than "regulatory necessity"

### 8.3 Certification Risks
