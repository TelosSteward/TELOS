## California SB 53: The Legislation

### Official Details
- **Name**: Transparency in Frontier Artificial Intelligence Act (TFAIA)
- **Bill Number**: SB 53
- **Signed**: September 29, 2025 by Governor Gavin Newsom
- **Enforcement**: January 1, 2026 (2 months away)
- **Full Text**: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260SB53
- **Dedicated Site**: https://sb53.info

### Scope: Who It Covers
**Frontier AI Models**: Models trained using >10^26 FLOPs of compute

**Large Developers**: Companies with >$500M annual revenue deploying frontier models

**Covered Companies**: OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Google DeepMind, likely Mistral, Cohere

### Key Requirements

#### 1. Safety Framework Publication
- Must publish comprehensive safety framework on company website
- Must be publicly accessible
- Must detail safety protocols, risk mitigation strategies

#### 2. Model Release Transparency Reports
- Standardized disclosure for each frontier model release
- Must include training details, capabilities, limitations
- Updated annually or when materially changed

#### 3. Critical Safety Incident Reporting
**To**: California Office of Emergency Services (Cal OES)

**Timeline**:
- Standard incidents: Within 15 days
- Imminent risk incidents: Within 24 hours

**Reporters**: Both companies AND public can report

**Annual Reports**: Cal OES publishes anonymized aggregate incident data starting 2027

#### 4. Whistleblower Protections
- Legal protections for employees reporting safety concerns
- Civil penalties up to $1M per violation for retaliation
- Enforcement by California Attorney General

#### 5. CalCompute Consortium
- Government Operations Agency must create framework for public AI compute cluster
- Goal: "Safe, ethical, equitable, and sustainable" AI development
- Framework due: January 1, 2027
- Must include cost analysis and funding recommendations

### Penalties
- Civil penalties up to **$1M per violation**
- Enforced by California Attorney General
- Private right of action for whistleblowers
