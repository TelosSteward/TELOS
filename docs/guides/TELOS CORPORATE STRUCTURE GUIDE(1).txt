# ORIGIN INDUSTRIES PBC - CORPORATE STRUCTURE GUIDE

**Balancing Mission Credibility with Commercial Flexibility**

-----

1. **Mission credibility** (for grants, academic partnerships, regulatory acceptance)

The key insight: PBC structure enables sustainable public goods funding through commercial subsidiaries—not despite them.

## OPTION A: SUBSIDIARY MODEL (RECOMMENDED FOR GRANTS)

### Structure

```
Origin Industries PBC (Delaware PBC)
├─── TELOS Labs LLC (wholly-owned subsidiary)
│    ├─ Develops: TELOS framework
│    ├─ License: MIT (open source)
│    ├─ Purpose: Public benefit R&D
│    └─ Revenue: Grants, donations, research contracts
│
└─── TELOSCOPE Systems LLC (wholly-owned subsidiary)
     ├─ Develops: TELOSCOPE instrument
     ├─ License: Proprietary (commercial)
     ├─ Purpose: Commercial deployment
     └─ Revenue: SaaS, licensing, enterprise contracts
```

### How It Works

1. **PBC owns both LLCs** (100% ownership as subsidiaries)
1. **TELOS LLC** operates under PBC charter obligations (open research)
1. **TELOSCOPE LLC** has commercial flexibility
1. **PBC board** ensures TELOSCOPE profits fund TELOS research
1. **Money can be made** via TELOSCOPE without violating PBC charter

### Legal Foundation

**PBCs can have for-profit subsidiaries:**

- Completely legal and common
- Subsidiaries can be commercially aggressive
- PBC board ensures profits serve charter mission
- Must document public benefit impact

**Precedents:**

- Mozilla Foundation (non-profit) owns Mozilla Corporation (for-profit)
- Patagonia (B-Corp) owns for-profit retail subsidiaries
- OpenAI (non-profit) owned OpenAI LP (for-profit) before restructuring

### Money Flow

```
TELOSCOPE Systems LLC Revenue
         ↓
   (Profit sweep to parent)
         ↓
Origin Industries PBC
         ↓
   (Funds allocation per charter)
         ↓
TELOS Labs LLC (R&D funding)
```

**Tax Implications:**

- PBC can sweep subsidiary profits tax-free (consolidated return)
- Allocates funds per charter obligations
- Board documents public benefit impact

### Advantages for Grant Applications

**For AI Grant / Emergent Ventures / NSF SBIR:**

✅ Shows PBC commitment  
✅ Money ultimately serves charter  
✅ TELOSCOPE can still be fully commercial  
✅ Clean narrative

**Pitch Template:**

> “Origin Industries PBC operates two subsidiaries: TELOS Labs LLC (open research) and TELOSCOPE Systems LLC (commercial deployment). All TELOSCOPE profits fund TELOS research—creating sustainable AI safety infrastructure. Grant funds go to TELOS Labs for foundational research.”

**This is the Mozilla model:** Non-profit foundation funds open source research via for-profit corporation revenue.

## OPTION B: LICENSING MODEL (RECOMMENDED FOR VC PHASE)

### Structure

```
Origin Industries PBC
├─ Owns: IP portfolio, trademarks, patents
├─ Licenses: TELOS framework (open/free)
└─ Licenses: TELOSCOPE to commercial entities

TELOS Labs LLC (Separate entity or subsidiary)
├─ Licensed by PBC to develop TELOS
├─ Operates independently
└─ Must open-source per license terms

TELOSCOPE Systems LLC (Separate entity - potentially VC-backed)
├─ Licensed by PBC to commercialize TELOSCOPE
├─ Pays royalties/revenue share to PBC (e.g., 10-30%)
└─ Commercial flexibility, can raise VC independently
```

### How It Works

1. **PBC retains IP ownership**
1. **Licenses** commercial rights to TELOSCOPE Systems LLC
1. **Revenue share** (negotiable %) flows back to PBC
1. **TELOSCOPE LLC** operates independently
1. **Can raise VC**, sell equity, exit—without violating PBC charter

### Money Flow

```
TELOSCOPE Systems LLC Revenue
         ↓
   (License fee/royalty - e.g., 15%)
         ↓
Origin Industries PBC
         ↓
   (Grant funding or contract)
         ↓
TELOS Labs LLC (R&D grants)
```

**Tax Implications:**

- Licensing fees are taxable income to PBC
- PBC can make grants to TELOS Labs
- More arm’s-length relationship
- TELOSCOPE LLC taxed separately

### Advantages for VC Funding

**For Series A and beyond:**

✅ VCs can invest in TELOSCOPE LLC directly  
✅ Don’t have to navigate PBC governance  
✅ Clearer exit path (sell TELOSCOPE LLC)  
✅ More separation = more commercial flexibility

**Pitch Template:**

> “TELOSCOPE Systems LLC has exclusive commercial license from Origin Industries PBC. PBC receives 15% revenue share. VC investment goes into TELOSCOPE LLC, which operates independently while funding ongoing research through license fees.”

## WHAT’S LEGAL (AND COMMON)

### Fully Permitted Activities

✅ **PBC owning for-profit subsidiaries**

- Completely legal
- Common structure (Mozilla, Patagonia)
- Board must ensure profits serve charter

✅ **Subsidiary being fully commercial**

- Can charge market rates
- Can raise VC funding
- Can pursue aggressive growth
- Can have separate cap table

✅ **Profits flowing to parent PBC**

- Tax-efficient (consolidated return)
- Documented public benefit impact
- Board resolution showing charter alignment

✅ **Different licenses for different entities**

- TELOS (open) developed by TELOS Labs
- TELOSCOPE (proprietary) developed by TELOSCOPE Systems
- Not a problem as long as charter served overall

✅ **Commercial subsidiary raising VC**

- TELOSCOPE Systems can have outside investors
- VCs invest in subsidiary, not PBC
- PBC retains control through ownership or license terms

✅ **Paying market-rate salaries**

- Employees can be well-compensated
- Competitive with tech industry
- Reasonable compensation doesn’t violate charter

-----

### What to Avoid

❌ **“Public benefit” as fig leaf**

- Can’t just say “we’re PBC” then act purely profit-driven
- Board must document actual public benefit
- Annual benefit report required (in most states)

❌ **Self-dealing without board approval**

- Transactions between PBC and subsidiaries must be fair
- Board must approve material transactions
- Document everything (meeting minutes, resolutions)

❌ **Abandoning charter mission**

- Even with commercial subsidiary, overall mission must be served
- “TELOSCOPE profits fund TELOS research” must actually happen
- Can’t just accumulate money without public benefit impact

❌ **Misleading grant applications**

- Must be truthful about corporate structure
- Disclose commercial subsidiary if relevant
- Show how grant furthers public benefit

## SPECIFIC SCENARIOS & ANSWERS

### “Can we make money without violating PBC charter?”

**Yes, absolutely.**

**You can:**

- ✅ Charge enterprise prices for TELOSCOPE
- ✅ Raise VC for TELOSCOPE LLC (if structured correctly)
- ✅ Exit TELOSCOPE LLC separately (in licensing model)
- ✅ Pay market-rate salaries to founders/employees
- ✅ Pursue aggressive commercial growth

**As long as:**

- Board documents public benefit impact regularly
- Reasonable percentage of profits funds TELOS research
- Annual benefit report shows charter compliance
- Overall mission is served (even if subsidiaries profit-seeking)

**The Mozilla Precedent:**
Mozilla Corporation (for-profit) generates ~$500M/year revenue, pays market salaries, operates commercially—all while parent Mozilla Foundation maintains non-profit mission.

-----

### “Can TELOSCOPE be sold/acquired?”

**Depends on structure:**

**Subsidiary Model:**

- TELOSCOPE Systems LLC is owned by PBC
- Can’t be sold without PBC board approval
- Acquirer would need to agree to continue funding TELOS research
- More complex exit, but possible

**Licensing Model:**

- TELOSCOPE Systems LLC is separate entity
- Can be acquired independently
- PBC retains license terms (royalty continues)
- Cleaner exit path

**Recommendation:** Start with subsidiary model (grants), convert to licensing model if/when exit becomes relevant.

-----

### “Can we raise VC funding?”

**Yes, in multiple ways:**

**Option 1: VC invests in TELOSCOPE Systems subsidiary**

- PBC owns 100%, VCs own 0% initially
- PBC can issue new shares in subsidiary to VCs
- PBC ownership dilutes but retains control/license terms
- More complex but preserves structure

**Option 2: Convert to licensing model, VCs invest in licensed entity**

- PBC licenses IP to TELOSCOPE Systems LLC
- VCs invest in TELOSCOPE Systems directly
- Cleaner cap table for VCs
- PBC receives royalty regardless of equity structure

**Option 3: VC invests in PBC directly (rare)**

- Some PBCs have raised VC (Kickstarter, Patagonia)
- VCs must accept PBC governance (public benefit prioritized)
- Less common, more complex

**Recommendation:** If VC becomes necessary, convert to licensing model first.

-----

### “What percentage of profits must fund TELOS research?”

**No legal minimum, but must be defensible.**

**Best Practice:**

- Document in board resolutions
- Annual benefit report shows allocation
- Reasonable percentage (e.g., 20-40% of net profits)
- Depends on growth stage (early stage may reinvest more in TELOSCOPE)

**Example Allocation:**

```
TELOSCOPE Systems Net Profit: $1M

Allocation:
- 30% to TELOS Labs R&D: $300K
- 30% to TELOSCOPE growth: $300K
- 20% to PBC reserves: $200K
- 20% to team bonuses: $200K
```

**Board Minutes:** “Allocation advances charter mission by funding open research (30% to TELOS) while ensuring commercial sustainability (30% reinvested).”

-----

### “Can founders/employees get rich?”

**Yes.**

**Compensation Allowed:**

- Market-rate salaries
- Equity in subsidiaries (if structured correctly)
- Performance bonuses
- Profit-sharing

**The key:** Compensation must be reasonable and documented.

**Examples:**

- Mozilla CEO salary: ~$2-3M (public records)

**Your situation:**

- Founders can receive equity in TELOSCOPE Systems LLC

- Everyone wins: founders, mission, public

## TAX CONSIDERATIONS

### Federal Tax Treatment

**Subsidiary Model (Recommended Initially):**

**Origin Industries PBC:**

- Taxed as C-corporation
- Can file consolidated return with subsidiaries
- Profits from TELOSCOPE Systems swept tax-free to parent

**TELOS Labs LLC:**

- Disregarded entity (single-member LLC)
- Income/expenses consolidated with PBC
- Grant income may be taxable

**TELOSCOPE Systems LLC:**

- Disregarded entity (single-member LLC)
- Revenue/expenses consolidated with PBC
- Commercial income taxable to PBC

**Effective Result:**

- Single tax return for entire structure
- Internal transfers not taxable
- Clean and efficient

-----

**Licensing Model (If Using Later):**

**Origin Industries PBC:**

- Taxed as C-corporation
- Receives royalty income from TELOSCOPE Systems
- Can make grants to TELOS Labs (if structured correctly)

**TELOS Labs LLC:**

- Could elect non-profit status (501(c)(3)) if appropriate
- Or remain for-profit LLC with grant income
- Separate tax return

**TELOSCOPE Systems LLC:**

- Taxed as C-corporation (likely)
- Separate tax return
- Pays royalty to PBC as deductible expense

**Effective Result:**

- Multiple tax returns
- More complex but more separation
- Transfer pricing considerations

-----

### State Tax Considerations

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Can a PBC be as profitable as a regular C-corp?

**Yes.** There’s no legal limit on PBC profitability. The difference is governance, not economics.

**PBC requirement:** Consider public benefit in decisions  
**Not required:** Sacrifice profit for public benefit

**Example:** If TELOSCOPE can charge $100K/year per enterprise customer OR $50K/year, the PBC can charge $100K as long as product still serves public benefit mission (enabling governance compliance).

-----

### Do PBC board members have fiduciary duty to mission or shareholders?

**Both.** This is the key innovation.

**Traditional C-corp:** Fiduciary duty to shareholders only  
**PBC:** Fiduciary duty to shareholders AND public benefit

**Practically:** Board must balance profit and mission, documented in meeting minutes.

-----

### Can we pay ourselves competitive Silicon Valley salaries?

**Yes.** Reasonable compensation is allowed and expected.

**Standard:** Market rate for comparable positions  
**Documentation:** Board approval, comparables analysis  
**Transparency:** Disclosed in benefit report (optional but recommended)

**Example:** If CTO at commercial AI startup makes $300K + equity, you can pay similar at TELOSCOPE Systems LLC.

-----

### What if TELOSCOPE becomes huge (e.g., $100M+ revenue)?

**Structure scales.**

**Small scale ($1-5M revenue):**

- Simple subsidiary model works
- Board oversight straightforward
- Benefit report documents impact

**Medium scale ($5-50M revenue):**

- May add independent board members
- More formal governance processes
- Could convert to licensing model for flexibility

**Large scale ($50M+ revenue):**

- Likely licensing model
- TELOSCOPE could be separate VC-backed entity
- PBC receives royalty, funds large-scale TELOS research
- Consider European subsidiary for EU market

-----

### Can the PBC go public (IPO)?

**Yes.** Several PBCs have IPO’d or are positioned to:

- Warby Parker (PBC) IPO’d 2021
- Laureate Education (PBC) public since 2013
- Allbirds (PBC) IPO’d 2021

**For TELOSCOPE:**

- If TELOSCOPE Systems grows large, could IPO as PBC
- Or Origin Industries PBC could IPO
- Shareholders buy into mission-aligned structure
- Benefit report becomes public disclosure

**Unlikely near-term, but structure doesn’t prevent it.**

-----

### What happens if founders want to exit?

**Several paths:**

**Scenario 1: Acquisition of TELOSCOPE Systems**

- If subsidiary model: Requires PBC board approval
- Acquirer must agree to continue funding TELOS research
- Founders receive proceeds from sale
- PBC uses proceeds for mission

**Scenario 2: Management buyout**

- New team buys TELOSCOPE Systems from PBC
- PBC receives cash, continues funding TELOS
- Original founders exit with payment

**Scenario 3: Licensing model exit**

- TELOSCOPE Systems LLC sold separately
- PBC retains IP, license continues
- Cleaner exit, founders benefit from equity

**Key:** Structure allows exits while preserving mission.

## APPENDIX: SAMPLE CHARTER LANGUAGE

### Origin Industries PBC - Certificate of Incorporation (Draft)

```
ARTICLE I - NAME

The name of the corporation is Origin Industries PBC.

ARTICLE II - PUBLIC BENEFIT CORPORATION

This corporation is a public benefit corporation organized 
under Subchapter XV of the Delaware General Corporation Law.

ARTICLE III - PUBLIC BENEFIT PURPOSE

The specific public benefit purpose of this corporation is 
to advance Privacy and Purpose as foundational principles 
of machine intelligence, through:

(a) Development and maintenance of open-source mathematical 
frameworks for runtime AI governance;

(b) Promotion of privacy-preserving measurement and oversight 
mechanisms for AI systems;

(c) Creation of institutional infrastructure enabling measurable, 
auditable, and controllable AI governance;

(d) Dissemination of research, tools, and methodologies advancing 
transparent and accountable AI systems for human benefit.

ARTICLE IV - BALANCING INTERESTS

In discharging their duties, and in considering the best 
interests of the corporation, the board of directors shall 
consider the effects of any action upon:

(a) The stockholders of the corporation;
(b) The employees and workforce of the corporation;
(c) The interests of customers and users of AI governance systems;
(d) The advancement of privacy protection and purpose alignment 
    in machine intelligence;
(e) The public benefit purposes set forth in Article III;
(f) The long-term interests of the corporation and promotion 
    of the sustainable pursuit of the public benefit purposes.

ARTICLE V - ASSESSMENT OF PUBLIC BENEFIT

The corporation shall provide to its stockholders, at least 
biennially, a statement describing the efforts of the corporation 
to promote the public benefit purposes identified in Article III 
and the best interests of those materially affected by the 
corporation's conduct.
```

## APPENDIX: SAMPLE BOARD RESOLUTION

### Initial Board Resolution - Subsidiary Formation

```
ORIGIN INDUSTRIES PBC
UNANIMOUS WRITTEN CONSENT OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS

Date: [DATE]

The undersigned, being all of the members of the Board of 
Directors of Origin Industries PBC, a Delaware public benefit 
corporation (the "Corporation"), hereby adopt the following 
resolutions:

WHEREAS, the Corporation desires to form subsidiary limited 
liability companies to advance its public benefit purposes;

WHEREAS, separate entities will enable focused research 
development (TELOS Labs LLC) and commercial deployment 
(TELOSCOPE Systems LLC) while maintaining unified mission 
governance;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED:

1. FORMATION OF TELOS LABS LLC

   RESOLVED, that the Corporation shall form a wholly-owned 
   subsidiary limited liability company under the laws of 
   Delaware, to be named "TELOS Labs LLC" (the "Research 
   Subsidiary");

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that the purpose of the Research Subsidiary 
   shall be to develop open-source mathematical frameworks for 
   AI governance, to be licensed under permissive open-source 
   licenses;

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Research Subsidiary shall be 
   the primary recipient entity for research grants advancing 
   the Corporation's public benefit purposes;

2. FORMATION OF TELOSCOPE SYSTEMS LLC

   RESOLVED, that the Corporation shall form a wholly-owned 
   subsidiary limited liability company under the laws of 
   Delaware, to be named "TELOSCOPE Systems LLC" (the "Commercial 
   Subsidiary");

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that the purpose of the Commercial Subsidiary 
   shall be to commercialize privacy-preserving AI governance 
   infrastructure, with revenues funding continued research and 
   advancement of the Corporation's public benefit purposes;

3. PUBLIC BENEFIT ALIGNMENT

   RESOLVED, that all activities of the Research Subsidiary 
   and Commercial Subsidiary shall be conducted in furtherance 
   of the Corporation's public benefit purposes as set forth 
   in its Certificate of Incorporation;

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Board shall review subsidiary 
   performance at least annually to ensure alignment with 
   the Corporation's public benefit purposes;

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that net profits of the Commercial 
   Subsidiary shall be allocated to support the Research 
   Subsidiary's open-source development, with specific 
   allocation percentages to be determined annually by 
   the Board;

4. AUTHORIZATION

   RESOLVED, that the officers of the Corporation are hereby 
   authorized to take all actions necessary to form the 
   subsidiaries, including:

   (a) Filing certificates of formation;
   (b) Adopting operating agreements;
   (c) Appointing managers;
   (d) Opening bank accounts;
   (e) Making initial capital contributions;

5. IP ASSIGNMENT

   RESOLVED, that all intellectual property developed by 
   the Corporation, its founders, and employees shall be 
   assigned to the Corporation;

   RESOLVED FURTHER, that the Corporation shall license 
   intellectual property to the subsidiaries as appropriate 
   for their respective purposes;

This Consent may be executed in counterparts.

Dated: [DATE]

_______________________________
[Director Name]

_______________________________
[Director Name]
```
