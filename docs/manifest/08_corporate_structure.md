## Overview

The TELOS platform will be operated through a multi-entity corporate structure designed to balance:
- Public benefit mission (AI governance infrastructure)
- Commercial viability (sustainability)
- Regulatory partnerships (trust and credibility)
- IP protection (asset security)

**Primary Entity**: Origin Industries PBC (Delaware Public Benefit Corporation)
**Supporting Entities**: 2 subsidiary LLCs for specific functions

## Entity 1: Origin Industries PBC

### Structure

**Type**: Delaware Public Benefit Corporation
**Formation Date**: Q1 2026 (target: January-February)
**Jurisdiction**: Delaware, USA

**Public Benefit Purpose** (as stated in charter):
> "To develop and maintain infrastructure enabling observable, measurable, and accountable multi-stakeholder AI governance, thereby promoting public safety, regulatory compliance, and demonstrable AI alignment across domains."

### Governance

**Board of Directors** (3-5 members):
- Founder/CEO
- Technical lead
- Independent director (AI safety/governance expertise)
- Independent director (regulatory/legal expertise)
- Optional: Investor representative (if funded)

**Board Committees**:
- Public Benefit Committee: Ensures mission alignment
- Audit Committee: Financial oversight
- Technical Advisory Board: Platform development guidance

**Stakeholder Accountability**:
- Annual public benefit report (required by PBC law)
- Quarterly stakeholder updates
- Open metrics dashboard

### Operations

**Primary Activities**:
1. TELOS platform development and maintenance
2. TELOSCOPE Observatory operation
3. IP ownership and licensing
4. Regulatory partnership formation
5. Research and publication
6. Open source community management

**Revenue Streams** (planned):
- Licensing fees (commercial entities)
- Partnership fees (pilot programs)
- SaaS subscriptions (production deployments)
- Grants (AI safety, research)
- Corporate sponsorships

**Cost Centers**:
- Development team salaries
- Infrastructure (cloud, API costs)
- Legal and IP
- Partnerships and outreach
- Operations and admin

### Team Structure (Initial)

**Q1 2026 Launch**:
- Founder/CEO (full-time)
- Technical Lead (full-time)
- Part-time contractors: 2-3 (dev, design, marketing)

**Q2 2026 Expansion** (post-funding):
- CTO (full-time)
- Regulatory Partnerships Lead (full-time)
- Senior Engineers: 2
- DevOps Engineer: 1
- Business Development: 1

**Q3-Q4 2026** (scaling):
- Total team: 10-15 people
- Engineering: 5-7
- Partnerships: 2-3
- Operations: 2-3
- Leadership: 2

## Entity 2: Origin Regulatory Services LLC

### Structure

**Type**: Delaware Limited Liability Company
**Formation Date**: Q1 2026 (concurrent with PBC)
**Parent**: Origin Industries PBC (100% ownership)

**Purpose**: Dedicated entity for regulatory partnership engagements

### Operations

**Primary Activities**:
1. Regulatory partnership formation
2. Domain-specific attractor configuration
3. Supervised pilot support
4. Expert review coordination
5. Compliance evidence generation
6. Regulatory liaison

**Why Separate Entity?**:
- **Liability Isolation**: Regulatory work has unique risks
- **Contracting Flexibility**: Some agencies prefer LLC structure
- **Accounting Clarity**: Separate P&L for partnership activities
- **Team Focus**: Dedicated staff for regulatory work

**Revenue Model**:
- Partnership fees (per engagement)
- Configuration services (per domain attractor)
- Pilot support contracts (monthly retainer)
- Expert review services (hourly)

**Team** (Q2 2026+):
- Regulatory Partnerships Lead
- Domain experts (contractors): 3-5
- Project managers: 2
- Technical liaisons: 2

## Formation Timeline

### January 2026: Planning

**Activities**:
- Engage corporate attorney
- Draft formation documents
- Define ownership structure
- Set up bank accounts
- Obtain EIN (tax ID)

**Budget**: $5,000 - $10,000 (legal fees)

### February 2026: Filing

**Activities**:
- File PBC certificate of incorporation (Delaware)
- File LLC articles of organization (2 entities)
- Adopt bylaws and operating agreements
- Issue initial equity
- Register with Delaware Franchise Tax

**Budget**: $2,000 - $3,000 (filing fees, registered agent)

### March 2026: Operations Setup

**Activities**:
- Open business bank accounts
- Set up accounting (QuickBooks/Xero)
- Establish payroll (Gusto/Rippling)
- Purchase insurance (D&O, E&O, general liability)
- Set up contracts infrastructure

**Budget**: $5,000 - $8,000 (insurance, software, setup)

**Total Formation Cost**: $12,000 - $21,000

## Legal and Compliance

### Registered Agent

**Service**: Delaware registered agent (required)
**Provider**: Harvard Business Services or CT Corporation
**Cost**: $50 - $300/year

### Corporate Records

**Maintenance**:
- Annual board meetings (minutes required)
- Shareholder meetings (as needed)
- Stock ledger (cap table)
- Annual reports (Delaware + other jurisdictions)

**Tools**: Carta (cap table management), DocuSign (signatures)

### Tax Compliance

**Federal**:
- Form 1120 (C-corp, if elected) or pass-through
- Payroll taxes (941 quarterly, W-2 annual)
- 1099s for contractors

**State**:
- Delaware Franchise Tax (annual)
- State income tax (if applicable)
- Sales tax (if SaaS in taxable jurisdictions)

**Accountant**: Engage CPA firm (Q1 2026)
**Budget**: $10,000 - $20,000/year

### Insurance

**Required Coverage**:
- General Liability: $1M/$2M
- Professional Liability (E&O): $2M
- Directors & Officers (D&O): $2M
- Cyber Liability: $1M
- Workers Compensation (when employees)

**Cost**: $15,000 - $25,000/year (total)

**Contents** (per Delaware law):

2. Standards used to measure impact
3. Assessment of progress toward goals

## Relationship with Subsidiaries

### Management

**PBC Board** controls subsidiary operations:
- Appoints subsidiary managers
- Approves budgets
- Sets strategic direction
- Reviews quarterly financials

**Operational Autonomy**:
- Day-to-day decisions by subsidiary managers
- Hiring within approved headcount
- Contracts up to delegation limit ($25K)

### Financials

**Profit Distribution**:
- LLC profits flow to PBC (100% owner)
- Consolidated financial statements
- Separate P&L for each entity

**Transfer Pricing**:
- Services between entities at arm's length
- Licensing from PBC to LLCs (if applicable)
- Shared services allocation (HR, legal, etc.)

## Exit Scenarios

### Mission Preservation

**If Acquired**:
- PBC structure requires consideration of mission impact
- Board must assess benefit to stakeholders, not just shareholders
- Super-majority vote (e.g., 75%) for mission-changing actions

**If Failed**:
- Assets distributed per bylaws
- IP donated to nonprofit or open sourced (per mission)
- Partner configurations returned to regulatory bodies

### Conversion

**Cannot Convert to For-Profit Corporation** without:
- 2/3 shareholder vote
- Independent director approval
- Public disclosure and comment period
- Delaware Secretary of State approval

**Protections**: PBC status is sticky, designed to prevent mission drift
