## Executive Summary

TELOS represents a novel approach to AI governance through **demonstrable due diligence** - a mathematically rigorous framework for measuring conversational alignment with established purpose. Our Phase 2 validation (56 studies across 3 datasets) provides empirical evidence of governance efficacy while transparently measuring both successes and failures.

**Key Differentiator**: We don't claim perfect governance. We measure actual impact with statistical rigor, establishing audit trails for AI behavior that address both technical alignment and governance accountability.

## Key Messages - Elevator Pitch Variants

### 30-Second Version
"TELOS is the first AI governance framework with demonstrable due diligence. We use mathematical measurement to track conversational alignment with established purpose, validated across 56 controlled studies. Unlike other approaches, we transparently measure both successes and failures, establishing audit trails for AI behavior."

### 2-Minute Version
"Current AI governance lacks measurability - we can't prove systems follow stated goals. TELOS introduces the Primacy Attractor framework: we mathematically define conversational purpose through LLM semantic analysis, then track fidelity in embedding space. When drift occurs, we use counterfactual branching to measure governance impact.

We've validated this across 56 studies with 66.7-81.8% effectiveness. Critically, we measure failures too - our ΔF metric shows when interventions help versus when they harm. This transparency is what makes TELOS demonstrable due diligence, not just another safety claim.

The framework is production-ready with a reusable validation pipeline. We've generated 67 comprehensive research briefs with complete audit trails. This positions TELOS as the first empirically validated governance measurement framework."

### Technical Version (for reviewers)
"TELOS addresses the alignment measurement problem through progressive primacy extraction: LLM-at-every-turn semantic analysis establishes a statistical convergence point (primacy attractor) defining conversational purpose. Post-convergence, we monitor fidelity via cosine similarity in embedding space.

This directly addresses documented LLM failure modes. Liu et al. (2023) showed LLMs exhibit U-shaped attention bias ('Lost in the Middle'), losing track of information in long contexts. TELOS implements continuous proportional control that maintains governance despite this attention decay.

Drift triggers counterfactual branching - we generate parallel trajectories (original vs governed) and measure ΔF (delta fidelity). This quantifies governance impact with statistical rigor. Phase 2 validation across 56 conversations shows 66.7-81.8% effectiveness with full ΔF distributions.

Key innovation: zero-context interventions during counterfactual generation isolate governance effects. This enables causal inference about alignment mechanisms. The framework generates complete audit trails - every intervention, every measurement, full transparency."
